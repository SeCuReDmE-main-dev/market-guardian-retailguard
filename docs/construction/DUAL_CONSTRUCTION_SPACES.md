# Dual Construction Spaces

## Jean-Sébastien Construction Space

This lane holds the human-origin part of the project: field experience, ethical boundary, naming, philosophical frame, neutrosophic theory, and local retail reality.

Artifacts in this lane may be rough, visionary, or exploratory. They are still valuable because they preserve why the project exists.

## Codex Construction Space

This lane holds the AI-origin engineering part of the project: interfaces, tests, source layout, validation rules, safety language, and implementation discipline.

Artifacts in this lane are not authority by themselves. They are proposed construction material and must remain reviewable.

## Integration Space

The integration lane records when a human-origin artifact and a Codex-origin artifact become one accepted output. Each integration decision needs a rationale, timestamp, and traceable artifact IDs.

The goal is not to erase the difference between builders. The goal is to make collaboration durable and inspectable.
